Frequently Asked Questions

How do I reset my server password? +

To reset your server password:

  1. Log in to your ServerRent dashboard
  2. Navigate to the Servers section
  3. Select the server you need to reset the password for
  4. Click on "Settings" then "Reset Password"
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions to set a new password

Note: Your server will need to be rebooted for the password change to take effect.

What payment methods do you accept? +

We accept the following payment methods:

  • Credit/Debit Cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover)
  • PayPal
  • Bank Transfers
  • Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum)

For enterprise customers, we also offer invoicing with net-30 payment terms after credit approval.

How long does it take to provision a new server? +

Most virtual servers (VPS) are provisioned within 5-10 minutes after payment confirmation. Dedicated servers typically take 24-48 hours to set up and configure.

You'll receive an email notification with access details as soon as your server is ready. You can also check the status of your server provisioning in the dashboard under "Pending Orders."

Can I upgrade my server later? +

Yes, you can upgrade your server resources at any time. We offer flexible scaling options:

  • For VPS plans: You can upgrade CPU, RAM, and storage with just a few clicks. Most upgrades happen instantly with a quick reboot.
  • For Dedicated servers: Hardware upgrades are available but may require scheduling maintenance windows.

To upgrade, go to your server details page and click on the "Upgrade" button. You'll only be charged the pro-rated difference for the remainder of your billing cycle.

What's your uptime guarantee? +

We guarantee 99.9% uptime for all our hosting services. This is backed by our Service Level Agreement (SLA).

If we fail to meet this uptime guarantee, you may be eligible for service credits. The credit amount depends on the duration of the downtime and is applied automatically to your next billing cycle.

For detailed information about our uptime calculations and credit policy, please refer to our SLA documentation.
